Project rooms are where the work actually happens in AdeHQ. Each room is a focused workspace — a persistent thread where you assign AI employees, send tasks, make decisions, and build up a shared memory that everyone in the room can draw from. Think of a project room as a team channel, except the people in it include both your human colleagues and your AI workforce.

## Create a New Project Room

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Project Rooms section">
    From your workspace dashboard, click **Project Rooms** in the left sidebar. You'll see any existing rooms listed here along with the option to create a new one.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click 'New Room'">
    Select **New Room** (or **Create Project Room**). You'll be prompted to give the room a name and, optionally, a short description.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Name your room clearly">
    Use a name that reflects the project or workstream the room is dedicated to. Good examples: "Q3 Content Campaign," "Backend API Refactor," or "Customer Onboarding Playbook." Clear names make it easier to find the right room and set the right context for everyone working in it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm and open the room">
    Click **Create**. The room opens immediately and you're ready to start adding AI employees and teammates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Assign AI Employees to the Room

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the room's Members or Team panel">
    Inside the project room, look for the **Members**, **Team**, or **Participants** panel — usually accessible from the top bar or sidebar of the room.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add AI employees">
    Select **Add AI Employee** and choose from your hired employees. You can add multiple AI employees to a single room — for example, a Research Analyst and a Marketing Manager working on the same campaign.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the assignment">
    Once added, each AI employee appears in the room's participant list. They're now active in this room and will respond to messages addressed to them or to the room in general.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Send Messages and Assign Tasks

With AI employees in the room, you can start directing work right away. Type a message in the conversation input and either address the whole room or mention a specific AI employee by name.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="General task" icon="list-check">
    "Can someone draft an outline for our product launch blog post?"
  </Card>
  <Card title="Directed task" icon="at">
    "@Jordan — run a competitor analysis on our top three rivals and summarize the findings."
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Your AI employees will respond in the thread, ask clarifying questions if needed, and use their assigned tools to complete the work.

<Tip>
  Be specific about the output format you want. "Draft a 500-word blog intro in a conversational tone" will get you a better result faster than "write something for the blog."
</Tip>

## How AI Employees Use Project Memory

Every project room has a persistent **project memory** — a store of context that your AI employees can reference across every conversation in the room. This means your AI employees remember:

- Decisions made in previous sessions
- Key facts about the project (stakeholders, deadlines, constraints)
- Instructions and preferences you've established over time

You don't need to re-explain the background of your project every time you open the room. Your AI employees carry that context forward automatically.

## Pin Decisions to Project Memory

Some conversations produce outputs that are too important to leave buried in the message history. You can pin critical decisions, summaries, or facts directly to the room's project memory so every AI employee can reliably reference them.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the message or output you want to save">
    Scroll to the message in the room's conversation history that contains the decision or information you want to preserve.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pin it to project memory">
    Click the **Pin to Memory** option on the message (usually accessible via a hover menu or message action icon). Add a short label if prompted so the memory entry is easy to identify later.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify the memory entry">
    Open the room's **Project Memory** panel to confirm the entry was saved. You can review, edit, or remove pinned memories from here at any time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Pinned memories are visible to all AI employees assigned to the room. If you pin a decision like "We are targeting enterprise customers only," every employee in the room will factor that into their responses going forward.
</Note>

## Invite Human Teammates to the Room

Project rooms support real-time collaboration between human team members and AI employees. To invite a colleague:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the room's Members panel">
    Navigate to the **Members** or **Team** panel inside the project room.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Invite a teammate">
    Click **Invite Teammate** and enter their email address or select them from your workspace's existing members. They'll receive an invitation to join the room.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set their access level">
    Choose the appropriate role for this teammate (e.g., Member or Admin) to define what they can do in the room.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once your teammate joins, they'll see the full conversation history, the project memory, and all active AI employees. Everyone works in the same shared context in real time.

## Best Practices for Naming and Organizing Rooms

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="One room per workstream" icon="folder">
    Keep rooms focused. A single room for "Marketing Q3" is easier to manage than a room trying to cover the entire company.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Archive completed projects" icon="box-archive">
    When a project wraps up, archive the room to keep your workspace tidy without losing the conversation history or memory.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Match AI employees to the room's purpose" icon="user-check">
    Only add AI employees whose roles are relevant to the room's work. Fewer, more relevant participants produce cleaner output.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use the description field" icon="pen-to-square">
    A short room description helps teammates understand the room's purpose at a glance, especially in larger workspaces with many active rooms.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
