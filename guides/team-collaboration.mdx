AdeHQ is built for teams — not just AI teams, but the blend of human judgment and AI capability that makes work move faster. When you invite your colleagues into the workspace, everyone shares the same project rooms, the same memory, and the same AI workforce. Human teammates and AI employees operate side by side in a single unified environment, with no context lost between them.

## Invite Teammates to Your Workspace

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Workspace Settings">
    From the main navigation, click on your workspace name or avatar and select **Workspace Settings** (or **Team Settings**). This is where you manage members across the entire workspace.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Navigate to the Members section">
    Inside Workspace Settings, find the **Members** or **Team** tab. You'll see the current list of human members alongside any workspace-level configurations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send an invitation">
    Click **Invite Member** and enter your teammate's email address. They'll receive an email invitation with a link to join your workspace.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set their role">
    Before sending (or when the invite is pending), assign the teammate a workspace role:

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Admin" icon="shield">
        Full access — can hire and configure AI employees, create and manage project rooms, invite new members, and adjust workspace settings.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Member" icon="user">
        Standard access — can participate in project rooms they're added to, interact with AI employees, and contribute to shared work.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the invitation">
    Click **Send Invite**. Your teammate receives the invitation and, once they accept, appears in the Members list and gains access to the workspace.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Teammates only see the project rooms they've been added to. Joining the workspace doesn't automatically give access to every room — room membership is managed separately.
</Note>

## Add Teammates to Project Rooms

Workspace membership and room membership are distinct. To bring a teammate into a specific project room:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the project room">
    Navigate to the project room you want your teammate to join.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the Members panel">
    Click the **Members**, **Team**, or **Participants** icon in the room's toolbar or sidebar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your teammate">
    Select **Add Member** and choose your teammate from the list of workspace members. They'll be added to the room immediately and can see the full conversation history from the moment they join.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Real-Time Collaboration in Project Rooms

Once your team is in a project room together, everyone works in the same live environment:

- **Shared conversation thread.** Every message — from human teammates and AI employees alike — appears in the same timeline. No one is working off a stale view.
- **Shared project memory.** When an AI employee references pinned context, they're drawing from the same memory store your teammates can see and contribute to. Decisions stay visible and attributable.
- **Live presence.** You can see when teammates are active in the room, so you know when a question you've directed at an AI employee has also been seen by a colleague who might weigh in.

<Tip>
  Establish a habit of pinning key decisions to project memory, especially during busy collaborative sessions. It keeps your AI employees reliably informed and gives teammates a fast way to catch up if they join the room mid-project.
</Tip>

## How Human and AI Work Complements Each Other

The most effective teams in AdeHQ use AI employees to handle high-volume, repeatable work while human teammates focus on judgment, relationships, and direction-setting. A few patterns that work well:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Humans set direction, AI executes" icon="compass">
    A teammate defines the goal and constraints in the room; the AI employee researches, drafts, or builds toward it and surfaces results for human review.
  </Card>
  <Card title="AI prepares, humans decide" icon="scale-balanced">
    An AI employee gathers options, summarizes tradeoffs, and flags risks — the human teammate makes the final call and the AI documents the outcome in memory.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Parallel workstreams" icon="arrows-split-up-and-left">
    Multiple AI employees handle separate tracks of a project simultaneously while human teammates coordinate at the top level, jumping into individual threads as needed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="AI as a always-on collaborator" icon="clock">
    AI employees continue working between human sessions, picking up tasks left in the room overnight so teammates return to progress rather than a blank slate.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
  Human teammates and AI employees share the same project memory, but only human members with appropriate permissions can modify workspace settings, hire new AI employees, or change approval configurations.
</Info>

## Managing Teammate Access Over Time

As your team changes, you can update access at any time:

- **Change a teammate's workspace role** from Workspace Settings → Members.
- **Remove a teammate from a project room** via the room's Members panel without removing them from the workspace.
- **Revoke workspace access entirely** by removing a member from Workspace Settings. This removes them from all rooms and revokes their login.

<Warning>
  Revoking a teammate's workspace access is immediate. They will lose access to all project rooms and conversation history at the moment you confirm the removal.
</Warning>
