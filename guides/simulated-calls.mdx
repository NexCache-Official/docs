Sometimes a conversation needs more than a typed message and a reply. Simulated calls in AdeHQ let you engage with one or more of your AI employees in a real-time, interactive session — a focused exchange that feels closer to a live meeting than a chat thread. Whether you're thinking through a strategy, running a practice interview, or delivering a quick brief before a big project, simulated calls give you a more direct line to your AI workforce.

## What a Simulated Call Is

A simulated call is a real-time interactive session with one or more AI employees. During the call, your AI employees respond in character — drawing on their assigned role, persona, and the project memory of the room — in a fluid, back-and-forth format that's faster and more dynamic than standard messaging.

Calls are not passive. Your AI employees can use their tools during the session, reference pinned memories, and adapt their responses based on what you say in the moment.

<Info>
  Simulated calls are tied to a project room. The context, memory, and AI employees available during a call come from the room where you initiate it.
</Info>

## How to Start a Simulated Call

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the project room">
    Navigate to the project room containing the AI employee(s) you want to call. Simulated calls are initiated from within a room, so the room's context and memory carry into the session.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select an AI employee to call">
    In the room's participant panel or toolbar, find the AI employee you want to start a call with. Click **Start Call** (or the call icon) next to their name.

    To call multiple AI employees at once, select all participants you want to include before initiating the session.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Initiate the call">
    Click **Start Call** or **Begin Session**. The call interface opens — this is a dedicated real-time view separate from the main room thread, designed for rapid back-and-forth exchange.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Happens During the Call

Once the call is live, your AI employees are fully present in the session:

- **They respond in character.** Their role, name, persona, and instructions all carry over from their configuration. A Marketing Manager stays focused on strategy; an Engineering Lead will speak to technical tradeoffs.
- **They use their tools.** If you ask your Research Analyst to pull up some competitive data mid-call, they can invoke their tools and surface results without pausing the session.
- **They reference project memory.** Anything pinned to the room's project memory is available to the AI employees during the call, so they don't lose context just because you've switched modes.

<Tip>
  Treat the call like a real meeting. Give your AI employees an agenda or a specific question to anchor the session. Open-ended calls are less focused and harder to make use of afterward.
</Tip>

## How to End the Call

<Steps>
  <Step title="Close the call session">
    When you're ready to wrap up, click **End Call** or **Close Session** in the call interface. You'll be returned to the main project room view.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review what gets saved">
    After the call ends, a summary or transcript of the session is saved to the project room. Key exchanges and decisions made during the call are captured and can be pinned to project memory for future reference.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pin important outputs to memory">
    If the call produced a decision, a direction, or a key takeaway you want your AI employees to remember going forward, pin it to the room's project memory. See [Working in Project Rooms](/guides/working-in-project-rooms) for how to pin memory entries.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Call transcripts are stored in the project room and accessible to all room members — both AI employees and human teammates. This ensures no one misses what was discussed, even if they weren't part of the live session.
</Note>
