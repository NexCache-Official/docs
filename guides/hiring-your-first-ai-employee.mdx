Hiring an AI employee in AdeHQ takes just a few minutes. You choose a role, give your new hire a name, write their instructions, and assign the tools they'll use to get work done — no engineering setup required. By the end of this guide, your first AI employee will be ready to take on tasks inside a project room.

## Before You Begin

Make sure you have an AdeHQ workspace set up. If you haven't created one yet, sign in at [ade-hq-eight.vercel.app](https://ade-hq-eight.vercel.app) and follow the workspace creation prompt.

## Hire Your First AI Employee

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the AI Employees section">
    From your workspace dashboard, navigate to the **AI Employees** section in the left sidebar. This is where all of your hired AI employees live and where you manage their configurations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click 'Hire AI Employee'">
    Select the **Hire AI Employee** button at the top of the page. A setup panel will open to walk you through the configuration steps.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a role">
    Select a role for your new AI employee. The role defines their area of focus — it shapes how they interpret tasks and what kind of output they produce.

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Marketing Manager" icon="bullhorn">
        Content strategy, campaign planning, copywriting, and brand messaging.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Customer Support" icon="headset">
        Responding to inquiries, drafting support replies, and escalating issues.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Engineering Lead" icon="code">
        Code review, technical planning, debugging, and architecture decisions.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Research Analyst" icon="magnifying-glass">
        Market research, summarization, competitive analysis, and data synthesis.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>

    <Tip>
      Choose the most specific role that matches the work you have in mind. A focused role produces sharper, more consistent results than a broad "general assistant" role — your AI employee will stay on task and develop a coherent working style.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Give your employee a name">
    Enter a name for your AI employee. This name appears in project rooms, approval requests, and conversation history. Choose something that makes it easy for your team to know who they're talking to — for example, "Alex (Marketing)" or simply "Jordan."
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write their instructions and persona">
    In the **Instructions** field, describe how this employee should behave. Think of this as their onboarding brief: what they should prioritize, how they should communicate, and any constraints they should follow.

    A few things worth including:
    - Their primary responsibilities
    - Tone and communication style (formal, concise, collaborative, etc.)
    - Any topics or actions they should avoid without explicit approval
    - Context about your company or project they should always keep in mind

    <Note>
      These instructions persist across all conversations. You can update them at any time from the employee's settings page. Changes take effect in new messages.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign tools">
    Tools give your AI employee the ability to take real actions beyond conversation — things like searching the web, sending emails, or running code. Select the tools that match the work you've scoped for this role.

    For a full breakdown of available tools and best practices for matching them to roles, see the [Assigning Tools guide](/guides/assigning-tools).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save and confirm">
    Review your configuration and click **Save** (or **Confirm Hire**). Your AI employee will appear in the AI Employees list and is immediately available to be added to a project room.
  </Step>
</Steps>
