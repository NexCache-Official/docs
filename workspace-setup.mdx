Your workspace is the central hub of AdeHQ — every AI employee you hire, every project room you create, and every teammate you invite all live here. Taking a few minutes to configure it correctly means your whole team, human and AI alike, starts on solid footing.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set Your Workspace Name and Settings">
    After logging in, open **Settings** from the sidebar (look for the gear icon at the bottom of the navigation). Under the **General** tab you can:

    - Update your **Workspace name** — this appears across the UI and in any notifications your team receives
    - Set a **Workspace description** to clarify the team or company this workspace represents
    - Review your **billing plan** and usage limits

    <Note>
      Your workspace name is visible to every member you invite, including any future AI employees listed in shared project rooms. Use something your whole team will immediately recognise.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Invite Your Team Members">
    Navigate to **Settings → Members** and click **Invite member**. Enter the email address of each person you want to add and assign them a role:

    - **Owner** — full access to settings, billing, and all project rooms
    - **Admin** — can manage members and all project rooms, but not billing
    - **Member** — can create and participate in project rooms, hire AI employees, and collaborate

    Each invitee receives an email with a link to join your workspace. They'll be prompted to create an account if they don't already have one.

    <Tip>
      You can invite teammates before you've hired any AI employees. Getting the human side of your team in place first makes it easier to assign AI employees to the right project rooms from day one.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Understand the Workspace Structure">
    AdeHQ organises everything into two main areas that you'll return to constantly:

    **AI Employees**
    This is your roster. Every AI employee you hire appears here with their role, assigned tools, and current status. Think of it as your company's HR directory — but for AI. You manage hiring, configuration, and tool access from this section.

    **Project Rooms**
    Project rooms are where work actually happens. Each room is a self-contained collaboration space. You choose which AI employees (and human teammates) are present in each room, and every conversation, decision, and action taken inside is stored in that room's persistent memory.

    <Note>
      AI employees are workspace-level resources, but they work inside project rooms. The same AI employee can participate in multiple rooms simultaneously, and their memory is scoped per project so context never bleeds between unrelated work.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Navigate the Sidebar">
    The left sidebar is your primary navigation tool. Here's what each section contains:

    | Section | What You'll Find |
    |---|---|
    | **Home** | An overview of recent activity across your workspace |
    | **AI Employees** | Your full AI roster — hire, configure, and manage roles here |
    | **Project Rooms** | All active and archived project rooms |
    | **Settings** | Workspace preferences, members, billing, and integrations |

    <Tip>
      Pin your most active project rooms to the top of the sidebar for quick access. Look for the pin icon when you hover over a room name.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Approval and Notification Preferences">
    AdeHQ is designed to keep you in control. Before your AI employees take any high-stakes or irreversible actions, they pause and request your approval. To configure how and where you receive these requests:

    1. Go to **Settings → Notifications**
    2. Enable **In-app approval requests** to receive alerts directly in the AdeHQ interface
    3. Optionally enable **Email notifications** for approvals so you're alerted even when you're not logged in

    <Warning>
      If approval notifications are turned off, your AI employees will pause and wait silently until you manually review pending actions inside the project room. Make sure at least one notification channel is active so work doesn't stall unexpectedly.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for a Well-Organised Workspace

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Name Project Rooms Clearly" icon="door-open" href="/guides/working-in-project-rooms">
    Use descriptive room names like "Q3 Marketing Campaign" or "Backend API Refactor" rather than generic labels. Your AI employees use room context as part of their memory.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Match AI Roles to Real Needs" icon="user-check" href="/guides/hiring-your-first-ai-employee">
    Before hiring, map out the actual roles your team needs filled — Researcher, Engineer, Analyst. The more intentional the role, the more useful the AI employee becomes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keep Your Team Updated" icon="bell" href="/account/inviting-team-members">
    Use the member roles to give teammates the right level of access from the start. Avoid giving everyone Owner access — use Member or Admin for most of your team.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use Simulated Calls for Kick-offs" icon="phone" href="/guides/simulated-calls">
    When starting a complex project, jump on a simulated call with your AI workforce to brief them, align on goals, and kick off tasks before diving into async work in the project room.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
