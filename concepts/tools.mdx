Conversation alone doesn't get work done. Tools are what transform your AI employees from sophisticated chat partners into active contributors who can search the web, draft and send documents, query databases, and take meaningful actions inside and outside your workspace. The tools you assign to an AI employee directly determine what that employee is capable of doing on your behalf.

## What Tools Are

A tool is a discrete capability that you grant to an AI employee. When an AI employee has a tool available, it can invoke that capability autonomously as part of completing a task — without you having to switch to another app or copy-paste results back and forth.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Web Search" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Find up-to-date information from across the internet to inform decisions, research competitors, or surface relevant context.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Email" icon="envelope">
    Draft, review, and send emails on your behalf — or flag them for your approval before they go out.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Document Creation" icon="file-pen">
    Generate structured documents, reports, and written deliverables directly within the project room.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Data Access" icon="database">

  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>

</Note>

## Matching Tools to Roles

Not every AI employee needs every tool. Assigning tools thoughtfully — based on the employee's role — keeps their capabilities focused and makes their actions easier to oversee.

<Accordion title="Marketing Manager">
  Typical tools: web search, document creation, email, social scheduling. A Marketing Manager uses these to research trends, draft campaigns, and coordinate outreach.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Engineering Lead">
  Typical tools: code execution, data access, documentation generation, issue tracking. An Engineering Lead uses these to review codebases, query system data, and maintain technical records.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Customer Support Rep">
  Typical tools: email, data access, knowledge base search, ticketing. A Customer Support Rep uses these to look up customer records, draft responses, and log resolutions.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Start with the minimum set of tools an AI employee needs to do their job. You can always add more as you build confidence in how they operate and what they need to be effective.
</Tip>

## How AI Employees Use Tools

AI employees select and invoke tools as part of their reasoning process. When you assign a task, the employee determines which tools — if any — are needed to complete it, calls them in the appropriate order, and incorporates the results into its response or action.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Task Is Assigned">
    You send a message or assign a task to an AI employee in the project room.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Employee Determines Required Tools">
    The AI employee evaluates what actions are needed and identifies which of its assigned tools are relevant.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tools Are Invoked">
    The employee calls each tool in sequence, using the output of one to inform the next where needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Results Are Returned">
    The employee synthesizes the tool outputs and delivers a response, completed action, or follow-up question to the project room.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Approval Requests for High-Impact Actions

Some tool actions carry more risk than others. Sending an email to an external contact, modifying a production record, or publishing content are examples of actions that may have significant or irreversible consequences. For these, AI employees pause and request your explicit approval before proceeding.

<Warning>
  Never assume a high-impact tool action has been taken without checking the approval queue. If an AI employee is waiting for approval, the action is on hold until you respond.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="AI Employee Flags the Action">
    Before invoking a high-impact tool, the AI employee surfaces an approval request in the project room with a clear summary of what it intends to do.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You Review the Request">
    Read the proposed action, including which tool will be used, what inputs will be passed, and what outcome is expected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve, Modify, or Decline">
    Approve to let the action proceed as described, provide modifications to change the inputs or approach, or decline to cancel the action entirely.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Approval thresholds can vary by tool. Some tools may never require approval; others may always require it. Your workspace settings determine which tool actions trigger approval requests.
</Info>

## Tools vs. Conversation

It's worth being clear about the distinction: tools are for **taking action**, not for having a conversation. An AI employee can answer questions, brainstorm ideas, and summarize information using its built-in reasoning without invoking any tool at all. Tools come into play when the task requires interacting with the outside world or your connected systems.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="No Tool Needed" icon="message">
    Drafting a strategy outline, answering a question about project memory, summarizing a conversation, or generating a plan all happen within the AI employee's reasoning — no tool call required.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tool Required" icon="plug">
    Sending that draft as an email, pulling live data to back up the plan, searching for the latest competitor pricing, or logging a decision to an external system all require a tool invocation.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Assigning Tools to AI Employees

Tool assignment is covered in depth in the guides section. For a quick overview:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the AI Employee's Profile">
    Navigate to the AI employee you want to configure from your workspace or project room.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Tools Tab">
    Open the Tools tab on the employee's profile to see all currently assigned tools and the full list of available options.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add or Remove Tools">
    Toggle tools on or off. Changes apply immediately — the AI employee's capabilities update in real time.
  </Step>
</Steps>
