AI employees are the core of AdeHQ. Unlike a chatbot you prompt from scratch each time, an AI employee holds a defined role, carries its own set of tools, and maintains memory of your project over time — behaving less like a script and more like a genuine member of your team.

## What Makes an AI Employee

Every AI employee in AdeHQ is built around three things: a **role**, a **toolset**, and **persistent memory**. The role shapes how the employee thinks and communicates. The toolset determines what actions it can take. Memory keeps it grounded in the context of your specific project.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Defined Role" icon="id-badge">
    Each AI employee has a specific job title — Marketing Manager, Engineering Lead, Customer Support Rep — that scopes their focus and expertise.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Assigned Tools" icon="wrench">
    Tools give AI employees the ability to act: search the web, draft documents, send emails, query data, and more.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Persistent Memory" icon="brain">
    AI employees remember decisions, facts, and context from previous sessions so you never repeat yourself.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How AI Employees Differ from Traditional Automation

Traditional automation runs predefined scripts — it does exactly what it was programmed to do and nothing more. AI employees are different.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Traditional Automation" icon="gear">
    - Executes fixed, scripted steps
    - Requires exact inputs and conditions
    - Cannot adapt to new information
    - Passive — only runs when triggered
  </Card>
  <Card title="AI Employees" icon="user-astronaut">
    - Adapts reasoning to the current situation
    - Draws on project memory and prior context
    - Takes initiative within their assigned role
    - Proactively surfaces blockers and next steps
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Where AI Employees Live

AI employees operate inside **project rooms** — dedicated workspaces tied to a specific initiative or objective. You assign AI employees to a room, and from that point forward they collaborate with you and your human teammates directly in that space.

<Note>
  Each AI employee exists within the context of a project room. The same AI employee role can be assigned across multiple rooms, each with its own isolated memory and conversation history.
</Note>

## Human Approval for High-Impact Actions

AI employees don't act unilaterally on sensitive or high-impact tasks. Before executing a risky action — such as sending an external communication or modifying important data — they pause and request your explicit approval.

<Steps>
  <Step title="AI Employee Identifies a Risky Action">
    During its work, the AI employee determines that the next step requires an action above a defined risk threshold.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approval Request Is Surfaced">
    An approval prompt appears in the project room, summarizing what the employee intends to do and why.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You Decide">
    Approve the action to proceed, request a modification, or decline — the AI employee waits and then continues accordingly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Approval checkpoints are especially useful when onboarding a new AI employee. They let you build confidence in how the employee operates before granting it more autonomy.
</Tip>

## Simulated Calls

You can jump on a simulated call with your AI workforce at any time. Calls give you a more dynamic, back-and-forth format for working through ideas, reviewing progress, or rapidly delegating tasks — without leaving AdeHQ.

<Info>
  Simulated calls are tied to the project room context, so your AI employees show up to the call already briefed on everything they know about the project.
</Info>
